About

For companies tired of consulting firms that bill by the hour and deliver by the year.

The short version

You have enterprise systems that don't talk to each other, approval workflows that run on email and spreadsheets, and integration projects that keep getting quoted in months when you need them in weeks.

We fix that. Working software, complete documentation, and knowledge transfer you can actually use.

The real story

— Nathan Murray, Founder

Before starting Forged, I spent years working inside enterprise companies—the kind that hire consulting firms for system integrations and process automation.

I've sat in the kickoff meetings where the timeline was "optimistic." I've watched 6-month projects stretch to 18 months. I've seen the parade of junior consultants cycle through while partners bill for oversight. I've been in the room when the final solution arrived and nobody knew how to maintain it because the documentation was an afterthought.

I got tired of watching good companies overpay for underwhelming results.

So I started building solutions myself—not as a career consultant, but as someone who understands the business problems firsthand. My first major project was a complete SAP-DocuSign integration: OAuth authentication, real-time webhooks, automated approval workflows. It took less than a week. Projects like that typically take consulting teams months.

That result wasn't because of some technical breakthrough. It was because I understood the problem well enough to solve it directly, without the overhead of discovery phases, requirements documents, and status meetings that exist mainly to justify billable hours.

Who we work with

We work with companies of all sizes that need enterprise systems to actually work together—without the overhead of traditional consulting.

You might be a good fit if:

  • You run SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, or similar enterprise systems and need to connect them to cloud platforms
  • You've been burned by integration projects that went over time and budget
  • Your approval processes still run on email chains and PDF attachments
  • You need someone who can understand your business problem and build the solution
  • You want working software, not a proposal that leads to more proposals

We've worked across manufacturing, aerospace, distribution, and more—but the problems tend to be similar everywhere.

How I work—and how I don't

I don't do endless discovery phases. I don't write 50-page requirements documents that nobody reads. I don't cycle junior consultants through your project while a senior partner checks in quarterly.

Instead: you describe the problem, I design a solution, we agree on scope and cost, and I build it. You see working software early and often. When it's done, it works in your environment—not just in a demo.

No change orders. No scope creep that benefits my invoice. Fixed pricing that means we're both incentivized to get it done right.

What I believe

Working software with clear documentation beats either alone. Code without context becomes technical debt. Documentation without working software is just theory. You need both.

Speed matters. Not reckless speed—thoughtful speed. The faster you have a working solution, the faster you get value.

Autonomy produces better work. When clients tell me what they need and let me figure out how to build it, everyone wins.

Complexity is the enemy. The best solution is usually the simplest one that solves the problem. I don't over-engineer.

Aligned incentives matter. When consultants bill by the hour, they're rewarded for taking longer. When I quote a fixed price, I'm rewarded for solving your problem efficiently. That's how it should work.

Why "Forged"?

Forging is about shaping raw material into something useful through heat and pressure. It's craft. It's taking business requirements—often messy and unclear—and forming them into working solutions.

It's also the opposite of theoretical. Forged things are real. They work. That's what I deliver.
