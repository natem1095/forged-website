About

We bridge business and technology

Forged from the trenches

I spent years in CPG manufacturing, working hands-on with SAP. Not from a conference room—I was in the warehouse, on the loading dock, in the quality lab with a hairnet and frock, observing operations. I mapped processes and configured SAP to work for the people actually using it.

When standard configurations couldn't solve a problem, waiting for corporate to build solutions meant watching teams struggle for months with half-baked workarounds. So I started building solutions myself—understanding the business problem and delivering the fix directly.

The real test came with our SAP-DocuSign integration—the kind of solution SAP sells as a product. The company had hired consultants who'd been circling for months. I designed and programmed the complete architecture from the ground up, single-handedly, in 4 days: 40 ABAP components, 15+ database tables, 16 documentation files, 2 business integrations, OAuth authentication, real-time webhooks, automatic PO release. The kind of project that takes consulting firms 6-9 months and $400K-$600K.

That experience shaped everything about how Forged works. The person who understands the problem builds the solution. No handoffs, no committees, no months of discovery that go nowhere. Just results.

— Nathan Murray, Founder

How we work

We don't do endless discovery phases. We don't write 50-page requirements documents that nobody reads. We don't cycle junior consultants through your project while a senior partner checks in quarterly.

Instead: we go to where the work happens. We talk to the people using the systems—not just management. We understand the actual workflow, the workarounds, what everyone wishes the system could do. Then we build it.

You see working software early and often. When it's done, it works in your environment—not just in a demo.

What we believe

Speed comes from removing layers, not cutting corners. Traditional consulting models add layers. Layers add complexity. We remove layers.

Working software beats proposals. Proposals can be impressive. Decks can be polished. But software either works in your environment or it doesn't.

Complexity is the enemy. The best solution is usually the simplest one that solves the problem. We don't over-engineer, and we don't build for hypothetical futures.

Aligned incentives matter. When consultants bill by the hour, they're rewarded for taking longer. When we quote fixed prices, we're rewarded for solving your problem efficiently. That's how it should work.

Why "Forged"?

Forging is about shaping raw material into something useful through heat and pressure. It's craft. It's taking business requirements—often messy and unclear—and forming them into working solutions.

It's the opposite of theoretical. Forged things are real. They work. That's what we deliver.
