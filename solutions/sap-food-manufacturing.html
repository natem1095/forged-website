Food Manufacturing ERP Implementation

Full SAP MM/QM/PM/PP implementation for food manufacturing—from greenfield plant to multi-site rollout with legacy system integration via IDOC.

Timeline: 6-12 months Platform: SAP ECC 6.0 Role: Functional Lead
4 SAP Modules
4 Plants Rolled Out
1000+ Materials
IDOC Legacy Integration

The Problem

A food manufacturing company with multiple production facilities faced a fragmented technology landscape. One greenfield plant was running entirely on spreadsheets and manual processes. Three to four existing plants were on an outdated Accuterm system with no real-time visibility.

The operational challenges were significant:

  • No lot traceability — Unable to track ingredients through production to finished goods
  • Manual inventory management — Spreadsheets, paper counts, no real-time stock visibility
  • Compliance gaps — FDA and FSMA requirements not being met
  • No shelf life management — FEFO (First Expired, First Out) not enforced
  • Siloed maintenance — Reactive breakdowns, no preventive maintenance plans
  • Quality blind spots — No systematic inspection or defect tracking
  • Production planning by gut — No integrated BOMs, routings, or capacity planning

The Solution

A comprehensive SAP ECC 6.0 implementation covering four core modules—Materials Management, Quality Management, Plant Maintenance, and Production Planning—with full integration to the legacy Accuterm system via IDOC interfaces.

Implementation Approach

  • Greenfield plant first — Clean implementation with no legacy constraints, used as template
  • Legacy migration with IDOC — Phased rollout to existing plants with bidirectional data sync
  • Process redesign — Stakeholder workshops to map current state and design SAP-enabled workflows
  • Change management — Training programs for end users and super users at each plant

  • Preventive maintenance plans
  • Work order processing

PP — Production Planning

  • Bills of Materials (BOMs)
  • Routings & work centers
  • Production orders
  • Shop floor confirmations
  • Batch management

Food Industry Requirements

Food manufacturing has specific requirements beyond standard ERP functionality. The implementation addressed these industry-specific needs:

Lot Traceability

Full batch/lot tracking from raw material receipt through production to finished goods shipment. Every ingredient lot is linked to production batches, enabling complete forward and backward traceability in case of quality issues or recalls.

Shelf Life & FEFO

Shelf life management with expiration date tracking at the batch level. FEFO (First Expired, First Out) picking strategies ensure oldest inventory ships first, reducing waste and customer complaints.

Regulatory Compliance

Configuration supporting FDA and FSMA requirements including:

  • Lot-level documentation and audit trails
  • Quality inspection at receiving and production
  • Supplier qualification tracking
  • Recall simulation and execution capability

Integration Architecture

The multi-plant rollout required careful orchestration between the new SAP system and existing legacy infrastructure. IDOC interfaces provided bidirectional data synchronization during the transition period.

Results

  • Real-time visibility — Inventory, production status, and quality data available instantly across all plants
  • Process standardization — Consistent procedures across all facilities, reducing training time and errors
  • Complete traceability — Lot-level tracking from supplier to customer, recall-ready in minutes not days
  • Compliance readiness — Audit-ready documentation and reporting for FDA/FSMA requirements
  • Reduced downtime — Preventive maintenance plans decreased unplanned equipment failures
  • Inventory accuracy — From spreadsheet guesses to real-time stock positions
  • Quality improvement — Systematic inspection and defect tracking enabled root cause analysis

Key Challenges Addressed

Process Redesign

Moving from spreadsheets to SAP required rethinking how work gets done. Extensive stakeholder workshops identified pain points and designed SAP-enabled workflows that improved efficiency while maintaining operational flexibility.

Integration Complexity

The Accuterm legacy system couldn't be replaced overnight. IDOC interfaces provided reliable bidirectional sync, allowing gradual migration while maintaining business continuity.

User Adoption

Change management was critical. Role-based training, super-user programs, and extensive documentation ensured users were confident and productive from day one.
